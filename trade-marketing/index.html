Case Study: Distributor Incentive Redesign

Most leaders talk about results. Few show how they think.
Here's one example of how I redesign a broken commercial system end-to-end.

Context
Bajaj Electricals — 140 distributors, 95 territories, South & North India zones. A partner network underperforming on margin despite meeting volume targets.
Primary outcome
20%
Annual revenue growth, sustained post-redesign
Time to impact
12 months
From redesign to sustained growth
Scale
95 territories
South & North India zones · 140 distributors
The problem
The incentive structure rewarded distributors purely on primary sales volume — how much stock they bought from us, not how much they sold through. This created a predictable failure: distributors loaded up on fast-moving low-margin SKUs to hit volume targets, ignored premium products, and gamed scheme eligibility. The channel was busy, but unprofitable.
What I changed
  • 1
    Diagnosed the misalignment. Mapped incentive payout vs. actual margin contribution per distributor — found the top 20% by volume were delivering below-average margins.
  • 2
    Introduced margin-based tiers. Redesigned payout structure so rebate percentages scaled with premium product sell-through, not just total offtake. Volume still mattered — but margin led.
  • 3
    Added eligibility thresholds. Distributors had to hit minimum sell-through rates on premium SKUs to unlock higher-tier rebates — making range-selling commercially rational for them.
  • 4
    Built monthly analytics loop. Tracked scheme burn rates, flagged underperforming accounts, and delivered corrective recommendations to regional heads monthly — closing the feedback loop.
