01 — Budget Forecasting & Planning Across 8 Departments

Allegheny County DHS  ·  Budget Analyst II

Eight departments, no consistent forecasting discipline, and a recurring pattern: budgets would run short mid-year, triggering reactive reallocation cycles that consumed weeks of leadership bandwidth. I built the forecasting framework from scratch — five years of historical spend data, variance analysis, seasonality modelling, and a stakeholder alignment process that made department heads co-owners of the projections. The 80% reduction in fund reallocations wasn't the goal; it was the evidence that the operating model had actually changed.

80% fewer budget reallocations 70% less manual effort in planning cycles

What I did

  • Pulled and cleaned 5 years of historical spending data across all 8 departments and cost centres
  • Built forecasting models incorporating spend trends, seasonality cycles, and variance analysis
  • Ran structured alignment sessions with department heads to validate projections and capture one-time policy-driven expenses
  • Established a proactive planning cadence that replaced the reactive fund transfer cycle

The outcomes

  • 80% reduction in mid-year fund reallocations
  • 70% decrease in manual effort and approval processing time
  • Faster, uninterrupted expense execution across departments
  • Improved financial governance and accuracy for executive-level allocation decisions
02 — Retailer Incentive Program — Rebuilt from the Ground Up

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Regional Channel Marketing Manager

The existing programme had 13 irregular 28-day cycles, tier-based bonus points that required a sales rep to decode, and zero self-service visibility for retailers. Participation had fallen to 15%. I redesigned the entire structure: simplified to a 12-month calendar, shifted to forward-looking performance targets so retailers could actually plan, and moved tracking to the retailer app so they never had to ask a rep where they stood. Participation went from 15% to 55% in six months. The structural change did what the incentive itself couldn't.

15% → 55% retailer participation 25% increase in SKU billing depth 15% sales uplift

What I did

  • Diagnosed the structural causes of low participation — complexity, opacity, and rep dependency
  • Transitioned from 13 irregular cycles to a standard 12-month calendar
  • Introduced forward-looking incentives based on prior cycle performance — retailers could predict earnings
  • Built self-service weekly tracking into the retailer app, eliminating sales rep dependency
  • Simplified qualification criteria to make targets motivating rather than confusing

The outcomes

  • Participation: 15% → 55% within 3–6 months
  • 15% sales uplift from higher retailer engagement
  • 25% improvement in SKU billing depth, strengthening product mix
03 — SKU Availability & Supply Chain Visibility

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Regional Channel Marketing Manager

Distributors were rationally optimising for fast-moving SKUs — the incentive structure rewarded volume, so they stocked volume. Mid and premium segments were perpetually understocked, and leadership had no visibility into territory-level inventory health. I built SKU-wise stocking norms based on actual sales frequency, demand patterns, and seasonality — then drove region-wide adoption across 95 territories over five months. SKU availability improved 45%. Incremental sales grew 15–18%. Not from better marketing. From fixing the inventory operating model.

+45% SKU availability 15–18% incremental sales growth

What I did

  • Built SKU-wise stocking norms based on sales frequency, demand patterns, and seasonality across 95 territories
  • Introduced minimum stock thresholds across an expanded SKU portfolio
  • Enabled data-driven replenishment cycles aligned to actual sales velocity
  • Executed region-wide adoption through distributor alignment over 5–6 months

The outcomes

  • +45% improvement in SKU availability across the distributor network
  • 15–18% incremental sales growth from reduced stock-outs
  • Recovered demand in mid and premium segments that had been systematically missed
04 — Distributor ROI & Channel Economics

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Regional Channel Marketing Manager

The problem was a misalignment baked into the incentive structure: sales teams were rewarded for revenue but not for collections, which meant working capital was chronically tied up in market credit. Distributors were profitable on paper and struggling in practice. I redesigned the incentive framework to reward both sales achievement and payment collection simultaneously — and introduced same-day payouts to make the behavioral shift immediate rather than theoretical. Distributor ROI improved 25–50%.

25–50% distributor ROI improvement Significant reduction in market credit exposure

What I did

  • Diagnosed the root cause: incentives rewarding revenue but not collections, blocking working capital
  • Redesigned sales incentives to jointly reward sales achievement and payment collection
  • Introduced same-day payout mechanics to create immediate behavioural shift
  • Ran range-selling training to improve SKU mix and inventory rotation at distributor level

The outcomes

  • 25–50% improvement in distributor ROI across the channel
  • Significant reduction in market credit exposure through improved collection discipline
  • Enhanced inventory rotation and product mix at the distributor level
05 — Crisis GTM — $2M in Revenue with No Budget

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Regional Channel Marketing Manager  ·  COVID-19 lockdown

The entire physical distribution channel shut down. Selling windows were three hours. Movement was restricted. Six months of aged premium inventory was accumulating. And there was no marketing budget. I designed a fully remote GTM motion in two weeks: WhatsApp-led campaign, product repositioning around anti-viral features, phone-based sales activation, and Google Forms for order capture across 4,000+ retailers. $2M in revenue. 25% of aged inventory liquidated in three days. Zero spend. The lesson wasn't about WhatsApp. It was about what a sales force can do when the operating model is redesigned around the constraints.

$2M revenue 25% inventory cleared in 3 days Zero marketing spend 4,000+ retailers reached

What I did

  • Designed a fully remote, digital-first sales campaign from scratch in under 2 weeks
  • Repositioned premium anti-viral fans as relevant COVID-era products — shifted the value narrative
  • Activated 4,000+ retailers via WhatsApp campaign with product demo video content
  • Enabled seamless digital ordering via Google Forms at scale
  • Coordinated sales team on phone-based selling across the entire retailer network

The outcomes

  • Reached 4,000+ retailers within the campaign window
  • Generated approximately $2M in revenue with zero marketing budget
  • 25% of aged inventory liquidated in 3 days
  • Built a scalable remote selling model that was replicated post-lockdown
