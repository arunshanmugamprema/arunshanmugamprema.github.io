02 — $40M Loyalty Programme Redesign — 15% to 55% Participation

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Sales Strategy & Operations Lead  ·  200,000+ retailers · 15 regions

National retailer participation sat at 15% across 200,000+ retailers. The organisation assumed this was the ceiling — without a bigger budget, nothing would move. I ran the diagnosis and found the real barrier wasn't incentive value; it was programme design complexity. Thirteen irregular cycles, tier-based points requiring a sales rep to decode, zero real-time visibility. I redesigned the entire $40M programme architecture: simplified to a 12-month calendar, built app-based self-service tracking, and managed rollout across 15 regions with weekly adoption monitoring. We hit the 18-month target in 6 months — participation from 15% to 55%, without increasing the incentive budget.

15% → 55% participation 18-month target in 6 months 15% revenue uplift 25% SKU billing increase

  • Diagnosed the real barrier as programme design complexity, not incentive value — redirected the solution accordingly
  • Redesigned the $40M loyalty architecture: 13 irregular cycles → standard 12-month structure
  • Built app-based self-service tracking, eliminating sales rep dependency across 200,000+ retailers
  • Managed rollout across 15 regions with weekly adoption monitoring
  • Programme model adopted as national standard following rollout success

The outcomes

  • Participation: 15% → 55% — 18-month target achieved in 6 months

  • Zero increase in incentive budget

03 — SKU Availability & Replenishment Discipline — 800+ SKUs

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Sales Strategy & Operations Lead  ·  200+ distributors · 15 regions

Distributors were rationally optimising for fast-moving SKUs across an 800+ SKU portfolio — the incentive structure rewarded volume, so they stocked volume. Mid and premium segments were perpetually understocked, and leadership had no visibility into territory-level inventory health. Sales loss from availability gaps was running at 28%. I built SKU-level minimum stock norms calibrated to seasonality and sales patterns, enforcing replenishment discipline across the entire network. Sales loss dropped from 28% to 11% over 3 quarters. The model was institutionalised as the standard planning system across all 15 regions.

Sales loss 28% → 11% 3 quarters 800+ SKUs covered Adopted across 15 regions

  • Enforced replenishment discipline across 200+ distributors and 15 regions
  • Built data-driven replenishment cycles aligned to actual sales velocity rather than distributor intuition
  • Institutionalised the model as the standard planning system across all 15 regions

The outcomes

  • Sales loss from availability gaps: 28% → 11% over 3 quarters
  • Recovered demand in mid and premium segments that had been systematically missed

Bajaj Electricals  ·  Sales Strategy & Operations Lead  ·  COVID-19 lockdown · scaled nationally
